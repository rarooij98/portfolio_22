JS mini-game

Web development (HTML, CSS, JS)

What was the project about?

I made a turn-based mini-game using HTML, CSS and Javascript. This game is loosely based on the open-world action RPG 'Genshin Impact' and involves 2 of their characters battling each other.

While making this game I used Javascript functions to execute the player's attacks, I used if-statements to check win-conditions, and I used a form to get input for the starting conditions of the game.

Try the game here if you like!

01

Flow

I started out by sketching the flow of the game

Before I started coding the game, I needed to know how the game would work exactly. I made a flowchart with all the steps the players could take, and when the game would end.

Then I wrote down each step in a bit more detail. For example, I needed to specify how much HP each attack would take. After that, I wrote next to each step how this could be expressed in code. I knew that for each Attack button, I would have to use an eventListener. And after each attack I would need to update the textContent of a heading to be the amount of damage taken.

02

Attacks

How do the players attack each other?

Each player has 3 attacks to choose from. Each attack can be used 3 times and you can only attack once per turn. But not all attacks are the same. The attack called 'Gust surge' for example, can do 30HP of damage, while 'Foreign ironwind' can only do 10HP.

Since this is a single-player game, Player 2 cannot choose their own attacks. For this player, one of their 3 attacks is randomly chosen, using Math.random() * 3.

03

Health

Each player has a healthbar on the top of their screen, this is where your HP is updated after each attack or heal.

At the start of the game you can choose your difficulty level: easy, medium or hard. This determines how much health you have compared to player 2. Normally, both players start out with the same amount of health: 200HP.

After an attack from your opponent, your HP is updated using this line of code: healthP1 -= 10. Your health bar will then shrink down. This bar is a canvas element, and its width is calculated by multiplying your current percentage of health with the starting width of the canvas. This means that when 50% of health is gone, the width of the canvas will also be 50% smaller.

The color of the canvas will also change depending on the percentage of health left. When at least 50% of health is lost, it will turn orange. At the same time, you will see the amount of damage taken displayed on your screen in green letters. I did this by updating the textContent of a heading to be the damage taken.

Luckily, you can also heal your character by clicking one of your healing potions. You start out with 3, each healing you for 10HP.

04

Win & lose

How does the game determine whether you've won or lost? And what happens after that?

Each time you attack your opponent, a check() function is called to check if their health is zero. If this is the case, you've won! The attack() function is immediatly stopped with a return statement. Then an overlay is made visible, telling you that you won and showing you a gif of your amazing character.

When you choose to rematch, the window will be reloaded with location.reload() so you can start all over again.
