AR biology book

XR, Unity, Blender

What was the project about?

Many students struggle to understand the abstract concepts and models in their biology textbooks. They also often have difficulty with model thinking: when students see a picture in their biology book, they think that the reality looks exactly like this model. Maybe XR can help to visualize these concepts?

My design challenge is: How could XR help students better understand the concepts in their biology textbooks?

During this project I made a prototype for an AR app that helps students experience a biological concept. By experiencing it themselves instead of only reading or looking at a two-dimensional picture, the lesson will be more memorable. With AR you can make biological processes interactive and show animations, because nature is anything but static. üçÉüå±

Concept

How did I use XR to solve the problem? ü§î

My concept is an AR enrichment of biology books for highschool students. The aim of this concept is to involve students more in the curriculum by making textbooks more fun, interactive and educational with AR. During this project I focused on the 'mimosa' plant. This plant is a common example in biology textbooks, because it is a great way to explain the difference between behavior and an impulse. When it is touched, it slowely closes its leaves.

When the teacher wants to explain this example to the class, they could use a picture in the book or a short video on the smart board. But with AR, students can experience for themselves what happens when they touch the plant. Nowadays, every student has a smartphone with them, and simply pointing the camera at an image in the book brings the image to life. Then they can see the plant as a life-size 3D object and when they touch it, the plant slowly closes, just like it would in real life.

05

Prototype

To give an impression of how this could be used by a student during biology class, I developed an AR interaction for one page of a textbook.

I made my prototype in Unity using Vuforia. To do this I had to define an Image Target and the 3D model that will appear on top of the target. That way, the mimosa plant will be on top of the textbook when you look at it through your phone. The picture I used as Image target is from the textbook BVJ havo 4. At first I used a free asset for my 3D model, but later I made my own model using Blender.

After that I wanted to add interaction and animation to the model. I did this by writing a few lines of C# in a script that I added to the ARCamera of the scene. This script states that when the user touches the plant, it will begin to shrink. When the plant is touched a second time, the scene is reset and the plant has grown back. Finally, I added some text above the model to explain what the user is supposed to do and how to restart the process.
