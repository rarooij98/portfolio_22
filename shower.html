Smart Shower

UX design, Arduino

What was the project about?

A popular way that people try to reduce their CO2 emissions is by taking shorter showers. But this is not as easy as it seems. The average Dutch person still showers for about 9 minutes, almost double the amount that is recommended by climate organisation Milieu Centraal.

To help people to change their behavoir, I designed a Smart Shower that helps you to save water.

This shower gives users a gentle push in the right direction, letting them shower 1L less every time, until they reach the recommended amount. While showering, the shower head communicates with colored light what percentage of your recommended amount of water you have already consumed. When more than 100% is used, the water turns cold to let you know that it's really time to get out.

02

Concept

This is how the Smart Shower works to reduce your water usage.

When you take a shower for the first time, a measurement is taken of your current shower behavior. The shower calculates how many litres of water you've used. This amount will be used as a starting point. The next time you shower, it will let you use 1 liter less. This way you shower a little bit less every time, until you are at the recommended level. This makes the transition a little easier.

While showering, the shower head communicates with colored light what percentage of your recommended amount of water you have already consumed. When 50% of the water is used, the light turns orange to warn you: last change to shampoo your hair! When 90% is used, the light turns red. Now is the time to start leaving your shower. When more than 100% is used, the water turns cold and the lights start flashing red.

03

Multimodal

During this project I was challenged to design a product that enables multimodal interaction. This allows the user to communicate with a product in multiple ways, using touch, speech, movement, sounds, light, color, etc.

The design of the Smart Shower supports this concept of multimodal interaction, because there are several different forms of user input en system feedback (see image, click to enlarge).

04

Persuasion

How does the Smart Shower persuade users to change their behavior? It uses the persuasive principles of scarcity and authority.

  1. Scarcity. You will use your time in the shower more purposefully, because you know it's limited. It's wonderful to daydream in the shower, but if you only have 5 minutes it might be better to start by washing your hair first. This prevents you from being in the shower longer than necessary.
  2. Authority. The Smart Shower is the authority that forces you to stop showering when your time is up. The shower also bases its behavior on figures from environmental organisation Milieu Centraal. The user is reminded of this whenever they're updated about their progress. As a result the user will respect the shower as an authority on sustainability and therefore they will be more likely to stop showering on time.
05

Prototype

I made a prototype to simulate the most important forms of feedback. For this I used an Arduino Uno with a LED strip, a servo and a distance meter.

The LED strip works with a timer (elapsedMillis). The color changes slightly every 60 seconds until after 5 minutes the lights have changed from green to red. The lights will then start flashing. After these 5 minutes the servo is used to turn off the hot tap and after 6 minutes also the cold tap. The distance meter is used to check whether the user has really gotten out of the shower. When the meter registers someone still under the shower, the servo is used to turn the tap off again.
